IB HL Visual Arts Show

Vicarious Odyssey

October, 2018

Clay, oil paint, yarn

15 x 8 x 20 cm

As the saying goes: “don’t judge a man until you’ve walked a mile in his shoes”. Vicarious Odyssey deals with hasty prejudices that people have of others. The shoe is moldy to indicate that it has been worn a great deal, symbolizing the depth of experiences the person has been through. The scattered faces represent the realities of a person’s life that most people don’t get to see on the exterior. This piece invites viewers to vicariously experience my authentic journey of growth and maturity.

IB HL Visual Arts Show

Trampled

November, 2019

Repurposed clothing sewn together

210 x 150 cm

Trampled is a testament of my multicultural upbringing. I sewed my old clothes together with a pedal sewing machine. I then went back in to hand stitch them to a blanket for structural support. This piece is meant to lay flat on the floor and stepped on like a carpet. As I often feel a disconnection to my 3 cultures (Chinese, Burmese, American-international school), Trampled represents my multicultural identity in a constant state of uncertainty as it is “trampled” over by others and myself.

Amidst Hidden Chaos

Octer, 2018

Acrylic, gesso, and stitching on canvas

88 x 69.5 cm

The ideal family dynamic in Myanmar exemplifies a perfect exterior. Amidst Hidden Chaos highlights my experiences with family dysfunctionality that opposes that ideal. The two figures in the painting are representations of my parents who are in conflict with each other. The face that is embroidered between the figures is me, as I am the glue that is holding them together, entangled amidst the chaos. This piece ultimately brings to light the unrealistic expectations of families.

IB HL Visual Arts Show

Picket Fence

May, 2020

Colored pencils, fabric, stitching

82 x 61 cm

Picket Fence displays the view from my bedroom window. Cooped up in that room during my darkest hours, I remember longing for better days as I stared out the prison-like window. On the other side of the window are accents of vibrancy and clarity that are visible but unattainable. As the outside world gets closer to my window, that sense of vibrancy and clarity are lost, leaving me hopeless and envious of a happier life.

IB HL Visual Arts Show

Apathy

September, 2019

Wire, fabric stitching, polymer clay, fairy lights, pipe, acrylic paint, purchased terra cotta pot that was painted with oil paint

69 x 55 x 60 cm

The romanticized lotus in Buddhism symbolizing enlightenment, rebirth, and detachment is juxtaposed by this 3D rendition through its unconventional materials. Wires and fabric are used to show grit, black is used to accentuate darkness of the mental state, and clay figures are used to highlight my detachment from the physical world. As the lotus is prominent in both China and Myanmar, my rendition represents personal struggles with culture and feelings of indifference and apathy.

Escapism

January, 2020

Acrylic on canvas

40.5 x 51 cm

As a daydreamer, I often find myself in a plethora of fantastical and abstract realms, escaping from my responsibilities. In Escapism, I wanted to represent this dream-like state because for me, exploring these realms helps me cope with the pressure of upholding expectations from myself and others. I believe that my imagination and inquisitive nature play key roles in how I choose to express myself in the real world, especially in the face of adversity.

Taking the Wheel

January, 2019

Acrylic on wood board

40.5 x 51 cm

This piece displays a resilient child. I made her very vulnerable, displaying the bruises on her face. The child is making direct eye contact with the viewer to provoke empathy and allow viewers to root for her as she “takes the wheel”—takes control of her life in the face of adversity. The vibrant and playful imagery represents her innocence which is contrasted by the brutality of her expression. I used a picture of me riding a toy car as a toddler as reference.

Memory Wall

February, 2020

Digital printed on cardstock

14 x 10.5 cm

Memory Wall is an interactive piece that asks the audience to write down a nostalgic memory in a card to pin up on the wall. As Joseph Conrad put it, “it seems to me that all my life before that momentous day is infinitely remote, a fading memory of light-hearted youth, something on the other side of a shadow”. This piece reminds us that growing up is a part of life. It helps us acknowledge the temporary nature of youth while still allowing us to revisit a time of hope and innocence.

IB HL Visual Arts Show

At Peace

October, 2019

Oil on canvas

45 x 45.5 cm

As an extension of Apathy, this piece is a self portrait set in a koi pond. In Chinese culture, koi ponds symbolize good fortune and serenity. Therefore, I wanted to depict the progression of my mental state from apathy to serenity through the contrast between two connected artworks. The pond is less detailed because I wanted to emphasize the peaceful expression on my face.
